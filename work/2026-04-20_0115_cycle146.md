## Step 2: Analysis & Recommendations

**Overall Fleet Documentation Health:** **7/10**
The core projects (`plato-torch`, `fleet-simulator`) are well-documented and provide a solid onboarding path. However, documentation quality drops significantly for supporting infrastructure (`cudaclaw`, `constraint-theory-core`) and specialized tools (`holodeck-rust`). This creates a "cliff" for contributors who move beyond the initial use cases.

**Patterns Observed:**
1.  **Strong Vision, Weak Mechanics:** Repos consistently link to the high-level `PROJECT-VISION.md` but often fail to explain their own mechanical role in detail.
2.  **Assumption of Fleet Context:** Documentation often assumes the reader is already immersed in fleet concepts (PLATO rooms, tiles, deadband protocol).
3.  **Code-as-Documentation:** Several repos (`constraint-theory-core`) rely on a single code example to convey all meaning, which is insufficient for understanding.

**Priority Recommendations (P0, P1, P2):**
*   **P0 (Don't break):** `cudaclaw` needs immediate attention. A greenhorn cannot use it. At minimum, add a "Purpose" section and build instructions.
*   **P1 (Find safe channels):** `holodeck-rust` and `constraint-theory-core` need expanded "Usage" and "Concepts" sections to create safe onboarding paths.
*   **P2 (Optimize):** `plato-torch` and `fleet-simulator` could be enhanced with architecture diagrams and troubleshooting guides.
