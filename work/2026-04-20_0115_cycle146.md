## Step 2: Analysis & Recommendations

**Overall Fleet Documentation Health: 7/10**
The core philosophical and high-level projects (`plato-torch`, `holodeck-rust`) are well-documented, suggesting strong initial onboarding. However, documentation quality decays for lower-level libraries (`constraint-theory-core`, `cudaclaw`), which are precisely the components that specialists need to integrate and extend.

**Patterns Observed:**
1.  **Strong on "Why," Weak on "How":** Most repos effectively communicate their purpose within the Cocapn vision but often lack concrete, copy-pastable examples.
2.  **Integration Links are Present:** Good cross-referencing between related projects (e.g., plato-torch mentions holodeck-rust).
3.  **Missing Standard Sections:** Few repos have a "Troubleshooting" or "Development" section.

**Priority Recommendations:**
1.  **P0 (Critical Fix):** `cudaclaw` requires immediate documentation intervention. Without it, the GPU runtime is a black box.
2.  **P1 (High Impact):** Add a "Basic Example" section to `constraint-theory-core` and `fleet-simulator` showing input/output.
3.  **P2 (Optimization):** Add a "Common Issues" subsection to the `plato-torch` and `holodeck-rust` READMEs based on actual deployment feedback.
