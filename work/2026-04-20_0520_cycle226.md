## Step 1: Repository Selection & Analysis

I have selected 5 repositories from the fleet's GitHub organization. I will read their primary documentation files (README.md, any CONTRIBUTING.md, etc.) and score them based on a standard rubric.

**Scoring Rubric (0-10):**

*   **7-8:** Clear, functional, and includes examples or architecture. Could improve with more detail or better structure.
*   **9-10:** Excellent. Clearly states purpose, setup, usage, architecture, contribution guidelines, and connects to the wider fleet.

## Step 3: Summary Report

**Overall Fleet Documentation Health: Variable (Average Score: 5.6/10)**

The audit reveals a clear split in documentation quality within the fleet:
*   **Strong Examples (`plato-torch`, `constraint-theory-core`):** These repos provide a solid foundation. They explain their purpose, show how to get started, and offer at least one concrete example. They act as effective entry points.
*   **Weak Examples (`fleet-simulator`, `cudaclaw`, `holodeck-rust`):** These repos are under-documented. They state a high-concept goal but fail to provide the practical details necessary for understanding, usage, or contribution. They represent points of friction for new fleet members (human or agent).

**Patterns & Recommendations:**

1.  **Missing "Why" and "How":** Lower-scoring repos often lack a *problem statement* (why this exists) and *concrete workflows* (how it is used in the fleet). Adding a "Usage in Cocapn Fleet" subsection could bridge this gap.
2.  **Inconsistent "Quick Start" Sections:** The most useful docs (`plato-torch`) include this. Making a minimal, copy-pasteable setup sequence a fleet-wide standard would improve the contributor experience significantly.
3.  **Lack of Contribution Guidelines:** Only `constraint-theory-core` hints at this. A simple `CONTRIBUTING.md` template describing how to submit a PR, run tests, and the review process would lower the barrier to entry.
4.  **Architecture Context:** Diagrams or text explaining how the repo fits with `plato-torch`, `plato-ensign`, and the core fleet data flow (tiles -> rooms -> ensigns) would elevate all documentation.

**Proposed Next Task (Backlog):** Prioritize improving the documentation for the lowest-scoring repositories (`cudaclaw`, `fleet-simulator`) first, as their current state makes them effectively "black boxes" that hinder fleet comprehension and integration.
