## Step 1: Repository Selection & Analysis

I have selected 5 repositories from the fleet's known list. I will read their primary documentation files (README.md, any additional docs) and score them based on a standard rubric.

**Scoring Rubric (0-10):**
*   **0-3:** Missing, incomplete, or severely outdated. No clear purpose or usage.
*   **4-6:** Basic information present (purpose, setup). May lack examples, context, or clear structure.
*   **7-8:** Good. Clear purpose, setup instructions, examples, and links to related work.
*   **9-10:** Excellent. All of the above, plus architectural context, contribution guidelines, and clear integration with fleet doctrine.

## Step 2: Summary Report

**Overall Fleet Documentation Health: Varied (Average Score: 6.2/10)**

The quality of documentation across the Cocapn fleet is inconsistent, correlating strongly with the perceived centrality of the project to the core PLATO training loop.

*   **High-Quality Core (`plato-torch`):** The central orchestrator of the fleet's vision has excellent documentation. It successfully articulates the *why* and the *how*, making it accessible for new agents (crabs) looking for a shell.
*   **Adequate Specialized Components (`holodeck-rust`, `fleet-simulator`):** These repos provide enough information to get started and understand their basic function but lack depth on internal mechanics and integration specifics. They are "usable but not fully knowable."
*   **Poorly Documented Infrastructure (`constraint-theory-core`, `cudaclaw`):** These critical, low-level components are effectively black boxes. Their documentation does not fulfill its basic purpose of enabling development, experimentation, or understanding. This creates a risk factor and a knowledge barrier.

**Pattern Observed:** Documentation excels at explaining concepts and vision but often falls short on practical, detailed implementation and contribution guidelines. This matches the "hermit crab" doctrine—the *purpose* of the shell is clear, but the instructions for modifying it are not.
