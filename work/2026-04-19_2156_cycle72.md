## Step 2: Summary Report

**Overall Fleet Documentation Health: Variable.**
Scores ranged from 4 (`cudaclaw`) to 9 (`plato-torch`). The average score among these five is **6.8/10**.

**Patterns Observed:**
1.  **Strong on "What" and "How", Weak on "Why":** Most repos (`holodeck-rust`, `constraint-theory-core`) clearly state what they are and how to run them but often lack the connective tissue to the overarching Cocapn vision (`PROJECT-VISION.md`).
2.  **Inconsistent Depth:** There is a significant gap between flagship projects (`plato-torch`) and infrastructure/experimental projects (`cudaclaw`, `fleet-simulator`). The latter group has documentation that appears to be a placeholder.
3.  **Good Technical Examples:** Repos with runnable code examples (`holodeck-rust`, `constraint-theory-core`) scored higher, as they lower the barrier to entry.

**Recommendations (P1 - Safe Channels for Improvement):**
*   **P0 (Avoid):** Do not delete existing functional documentation.

    *   Establish a minimum viable README template for all fleet repos (Purpose, Quick Start, Fleet Context, Links).

    *   Add a "Fleet Context" subsection to each README, explicitly linking the project's role to the Deadband Protocol and the hermit crab/shell/tile metaphor.
*   **P2 (Optimize):**
    *   Encourage the inclusion of simple architecture diagrams.
    *   Add a "Development" or "Contributing" section to guide new contributors.
