## Step 2: Overall Documentation Assessment & Patterns

**Average Score:** 6.0/10

**Patterns Observed:**
1.  **Internal Knowledge Assumed:** Documentation often assumes the reader is already immersed in fleet concepts (Deadband Protocol, tiles, PLATO rooms). This is effective for intra-fleet work but a barrier to external contributors.
2.  **Practical over Theoretical:** Most READMEs prioritize "how to run" over "why it exists" or "how it fits." This aligns with the fleet's pragmatic, build-and-test culture.
3.  **Inconsistent Depth:** Critical infrastructure (`plato-torch`) is well-documented. Supporting or research-focused repositories (`fleet-simulator`, `cudaclaw`) have minimal documentation, creating knowledge silos.
4.  **Lacking Cross-Links:** Few repositories reference each other. The connective tissue of the fleet architecture is not documented in the repos themselves; it exists primarily in the central `fleet-knowledge/` and in agents' context.

**Critical Gaps Identified:**
- `fleet-simulator` is the most severe case. Its purpose is fundamental (generating training data), yet its documentation is virtually absent.
- No repository included a standardized "Project Health" or "Service Status" section.
- Visual architecture diagrams are missing across the board.
