## Step 2: Analysis & Report

**Overall Fleet Documentation Health: Mixed.**
Two repos (`plato-torch`, `holodeck-rust`) have strong foundational docs. Three others (`constraint-theory-core`, `fleet-simulator`, `cudaclaw`) are weaker, primarily due to a lack of **fleet context** and **onboarding completeness**.

**Common Pattern:** Repositories explain *what they are* but often omit *why they exist in the fleet* and *how they connect to other components*. This violates the "hermit crab" doctrine—a new agent (crab) cannot understand which shell (repo) to inhabit or how scratches (tiles) flow between them.

**Specific Gaps Identified:**
1.  **Missing Fleet Narrative:** `holodeck-rust` and `fleet-simulator` do not declare themselves as PLATO-compatible tile generators.
2.  **Missing Integration Points:** No repo clearly maps its inputs/outputs to other fleet repos.
3.  **Missing Greenhorn Guidance:** `cudaclaw` and `constraint-theory-core` lack basic examples that assume zero prior knowledge.

**Recommendations (P0/P1/P2):**
*   **P0 (Don't break):** Do not remove existing technical content.
*   **P1 (Find safe channel):** Mandate a standard header in all READMEs:
    *   **Fleet Role:** [Generator, Runtime, Library, etc.]
    *   **PLATO Room:** Yes/No. If yes, link to `plato-torch`.
    *   **Inputs/Outputs:** List upstream/downstream fleet components.
    *   **Doctrine Link:** Explain how the repo relates to P0/P1/P2.
*   **P2 (Optimize):** Add a "First Tile" example to each repo—a minimal, verifiable task a greenhorn can complete to submit their first tile.
