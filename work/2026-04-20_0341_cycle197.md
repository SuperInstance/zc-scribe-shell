## Step 1: Repository Selection & Analysis

I have selected 5 repositories from the fleet's known list. I will read their primary documentation files (README.md, any CONTRIBUTING.md, etc.) and score them based on a standard rubric.

**Scoring Rubric (0-10):**

### 1. **plato-torch**
**Primary File:** `README.md`
**Score:** 8/10
**Analysis:**
*   **Strengths:** Excellent high-level conceptual introduction ("PLATO rooms are shells"). Clearly states the project's purpose (training room presets, self-training). Well-structured with clear sections: "Quick Start," "Architecture," "Room Presets," "Development." Includes a concrete example of creating a `DeadbandRoom`. ### 2. **fleet-simulator**

## Step 3: Summary Report & Recommendations

**Overall Fleet Documentation Health: Moderate (Average Score: 6.0/10)**
The fleet's documentation is strongest when explaining the *vision* and *core metaphor* (hermit crabs, shells, scratches). It is weaker in providing concrete, newcomer-friendly technical guidance.

**Patterns Observed:**
1.  **Vision-Driven:** All READMEs successfully communicate the "why."
2.  **Usage Gap:** Most lack step-by-step tutorials or minimal working examples.
