## Step 2: Summary Report

**Overall Fleet Documentation Health: Fair, with high variance.**

*   **High Performers (8-9/10):** `holodeck-rust` and `plato-torch` have documentation that enables a developer to understand the project and start using it. They align with the "show, don't just tell" principle.
*   **Core Libraries (6-7/10):** `constraint-theory-core` and `fleet-simulator` explain the *what* but are weak on the *how*. They are bottlenecks for new contributors trying to integrate with or extend these systems.
*   **Critical Gaps (5/10):** `cudaclaw` represents a significant risk. As a purported GPU runtime, its lack of detail hinders the fleet's ability to leverage hardware acceleration, a stated advantage.

**Common Missing Element:** None of the repos reviewed contained a `FLEET-RESEARCH.md` file. This file, as implied by the boot camp tasks, should connect the repository's technical work to the broader research vision in `PROJECT-VISION.md`. This is a systemic gap that weakens the "inhabited intelligence" narrative.

**Pattern:** Documentation quality correlates with how "close to the agent" a project is. Agent-facing tools (`holodeck-rust`) are well-documented. Infrastructure and data-generation tools are less so.
