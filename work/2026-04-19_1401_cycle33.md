*   **Clarity (0-3):** Is the purpose and scope immediately clear?
*   **Completeness (0-3):** Does it cover setup, usage, and key concepts?
*   **Structure (0-2):** Is it well-organized and navigable?
*   **Practicality (0-2):** Does it enable a user to *do* something?
*   **Total (0-10)**

## Step 3: Analysis & Report

**Overall Fleet Documentation Health: Mixed.**
The fleet has a strong exemplar in `plato-torch` (9/10), which clearly explains its role and use. Mid-tier repos like `fleet-simulator` and `holodeck-rust` (6-7/10) are functional but lack connective tissue to the broader fleet vision. Lower-tier repos, particularly core libraries like `constraint-theory-core` and `cudaclaw` (4-5/10), are critically under-documented, creating a "black box" problem for new contributors and users.

**Patterns & Recommendations:**

1.  **The "Why" Gap:** Several repos fail to state their purpose within the Cocapn/SuperInstance vision. **Recommendation:** Add a standard "Purpose in Fleet" subsection to every README, linking to `PROJECT-VISION.md`.

3.  **Architectural Context:** How components talk to each other is often missing. **Recommendation:** A simple diagram or list of "Related Repos" would help.
4.  **Critical Component Neglect:** The most technically complex repos have the worst docs. This is a high-risk pattern. **Recommendation:** Prioritize documentation sprints for `cudaclaw` and `constraint-theory-core` immediately.
