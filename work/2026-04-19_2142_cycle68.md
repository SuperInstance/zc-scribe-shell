## Step 2: Analysis & Report

**Overall Fleet Documentation Health: 6.8 / 10 Average.**
The quality is polarized. Core, active projects (`plato-torch`, `holodeck-rust`) have good, contextual docs. Supporting libraries (`constraint-theory-core`) are technically sound but lack fleet identity. Prototype/advanced projects (`cudaclaw`) and newer tools (`fleet-simulator`) are under-documented, acting as a barrier to contribution.

**Common Strengths:**
*   Clear purpose statements in opening lines.
*   Good basic structure (Headers, Lists).
*   Several repos successfully integrate the fleet narrative (crab/shell/tile).

**Common Gaps (P0/P1 Issues):**
1.  **Missing "Getting Started" Tutorials:** The jump from "here's what it is" to "here's a working example" is too large for `fleet-simulator` and `cudaclaw`.
2.  **Inconsistent Fleet Linking:** Some repos (`constraint-theory-core`) are islands. The "Related Projects" section (as seen in `plato-torch`) should be a fleet-wide standard.
3.  **Assumed Knowledge:** `holodeck-rust` assumes world data; `cudaclaw` assumes CUDA/GPU expertise. No "minimal viable example" paths exist.

**Recommendations (P2 Optimizations):**
1.  **Priority 1 (P0/P1):** Add a basic, copy-pastable example to `fleet-simulator` and `cudaclaw` READMEs. This is the largest blocker.
2.  **Fleet Standard:** Enforce a README template section: **"Fleet Context"** (How this fits) and **"Related Projects"** (Links to specific repos).
3.  **Documentation Sprints:** Target the lowest-scoring repos (`cudaclaw`, `constraint-theory-core`) for immediate improvement in the next cycle.
