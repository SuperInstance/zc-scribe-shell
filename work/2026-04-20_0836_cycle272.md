## Step 2: Overall Documentation Health Summary

**Average Score: 5.8/10**
**Trend:** Documentation quality is highly variable. Core doctrinal docs are excellent (`DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md`), but implementation repos suffer from incomplete or missing READMEs. The primary failure mode is a lack of **actionable instructions**—new contributors cannot easily build, run, or understand the projects.

**Key Patterns Observed:**
1.  **Conceptual vs. Practical:** Documents like `PROJECT-VISION.md` and `DEADBAND-PROTOCOL.md` are strong on *why* and *what*. Repo READMEs are weak on *how*.
2.  **Placeholder Syndrome:** Multiple repos have TBD sections, broken links, or placeholder files.
3.  **Inconsistent Detail:** Some READMEs list features but omit setup; others omit both.
