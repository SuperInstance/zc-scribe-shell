## Step 2: Documentation Analysis & Scoring

**Scoring Rubric (0-10):**
*   **0-3:** Missing or severely deficient. No purpose, setup, or usage.
*   **4-6:** Basic. Has core sections (purpose, install, run) but is unclear, incomplete, or outdated.
*   **7-8:** Good. Clear, structured, covers main use cases. May lack advanced details or context.
*   **9-10:** Excellent. Comprehensive, includes vision, architecture, examples, and connects to fleet doctrine.

### 1. cudaclaw
**Score: 5/10**
**Analysis:**
*   **Strengths:** Clearly states its purpose as a "GPU-resident agent runtime." Provides a concrete `Dockerfile` example for building and running, which is actionable.
*   **Weaknesses:** Very brief. Lacks any architectural context, explanation of key concepts (what is a "GPU-resident agent"?), or integration points with other fleet components. No mention of the Deadband Protocol or its role in the fleet. It reads like a technical note, not onboarding documentation.

### 2. plato-torch

## Step 3: Summary Report

**Overall Fleet Documentation Health: Variable (Average Score: 6.0/10)**

**Patterns Observed:**
1.  **Core vs. Peripheral:** Core systems like `plato-torch` have good documentation. Supporting tools (`fleet-simulator`, `constraint-theory-core`) are weaker.
2.  **Actionable vs. Conceptual:** Most repos provide enough to *run* something (Docker commands) but lack the conceptual framework to understand *why* it exists in the fleet.
3.  **Missing Links:** A major gap is the lack of cross-references between related projects. Documentation exists in silos.

**P0/P1/P2 Analysis:**
*   **P0 (Don't hit rocks):** The basic "how to run" is often covered, preventing immediate failure.
*   **P1 (Find safe channels):** This is the biggest gap. Documentation rarely maps the "safe channels" between repositories, leaving a contributor unsure how components fit together.
*   **P2 (Optimize):** Some repos (`plato-torch`) begin to optimize by providing architecture context.

**Recommendations (Scribe Backlog):**
1.  **Primary (P1):** Create a **FLEET-ARCHITECTURE.md** document that maps all key repositories, data flows (e.g., `holodeck-rust` -> `plato-torch` -> `plato-ensign`), and defines core shared terminology (Tile, Room, Ensign, Deadband).
2.  **Secondary:** Improve the three lowest-scoring READMEs (`fleet-simulator`, `cudaclaw`, `constraint-theory-core`) by adding:
    *   A "Purpose in Fleet" section.
    *   A "Connections" section linking to related repos.
    *   Clear, copy-paste setup instructions.
3.  **Tertiary (P2):** Add a standard "Documentation Quality" checklist to the Scribe role guidelines to ensure future READMEs meet a minimum standard.
