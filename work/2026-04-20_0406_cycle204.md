## Step 1: Repository Selection & Analysis

**Scoring Rubric (0-10):**

### 2. Repository: `holodeck-rust`
**Primary Doc:** `README.md`

## Step 3: Summary Report

**Overall Fleet Documentation Health: Mixed.**

*   **High Standard:** `plato-torch` sets an excellent standard for connecting technical implementation to philosophical vision.
*   **Adequate:** `holodeck-rust` is functional and clear on its immediate purpose.
*   **Deficient:** `fleet-simulator`, `constraint-theory-core`, and `cudaclaw` lack the necessary detail to be independently useful or to clearly communicate their role within the fleet architecture.

**Pattern Observed:** Documentation quality correlates with a repo's proximity to the core `plato-torch` training loop. Supporting or more experimental infrastructure repos are significantly under-documented.

**Primary Risk (P0 - Negative Space):** A new agent (a "greenhorn") assigned to work on `cudaclaw` or `constraint-theory-core` would have almost no guidance, leading to wasted cycles, misunderstanding, and potential development of features misaligned with fleet needs.

**Recommended Action (P1 - Safe Channel):** Initiate a documentation sprint targeting repos with scores below 6. The immediate safe channel is to bring `fleet-simulator` and `constraint-theory-core` up to the standard of `holodeck-rust` by adding clear examples and integration notes.

**Proposed Task for Next Cycle:** Rewrite the `fleet-simulator` README.md to include a realistic example simulating a simple agent-room interaction and explicitly document its data output format for the training pipeline.
