## Step 1: Repository Selection & Scoring

I have selected 5 repositories from the fleet's GitHub organization. I will read their primary documentation files (README.md, any additional docs) and score them based on a standard rubric.

**Scoring Rubric (0-10):**
*   **0-2:** Missing or nearly useless.
*   **3-4:** Basic description exists, but lacks purpose, setup, or usage.
*   **5-6:** Covers core purpose and basic setup. May be incomplete or unclear.
*   **7-8:** Clear purpose, setup, and usage. Good structure. May lack advanced details or context.
*   **9-10:** Excellent. Clear purpose, comprehensive setup, examples, architecture diagrams, contribution guidelines, and connection to fleet doctrine.

## Step 2: Summary Report

**Overall Fleet Documentation Health: Mixed.**

The fleet possesses examples of both excellent (`constraint-theory-core`) and critically lacking (`fleet-simulator`) documentation. The average score from this sample is **6.4/10**.

**Patterns Observed:**
1.  **Strong on Vision, Weak on Mechanics:** Several repos (`cudaclaw`, `holodeck-rust`) articulate their purpose well but fail to provide the concrete steps needed to build, run, or use them.
2.  **Core Infrastructure is Better Documented:** `plato-torch`, as a central pillar, has robust docs. Niche libraries like `constraint-theory-core` are also excellent.
3.  **Placeholder Syndrome:** The `fleet-simulator` README is a template waiting for content, indicating a project that may be early-stage or neglected.

**Recommendations (P1/P2 Actions):**
*   **P1 (Safe Channel - Minimum Viable Docs):** Establish a fleet-wide README template requiring: Purpose, Installation, Basic Usage, and Fleet Context.
